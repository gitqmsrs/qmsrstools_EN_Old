Use of Language Policy
Statement of Philosophy
At QMSrs, we use language as a medium of inquiry, providing opportunities to challenge, nurture
and develop both first and second language speakers. Language does not operate in isolation but in
the context of human experience and communication. Learning language is integral to the
development of personal, cognitive, social and cultural identity as well as to making meaning of
the world around us. QMSrs recognises that language is the foundation of all learning. Learners learn
language, learn through language, and learn about language.
We believe that learners bring to any learning situation their prior knowledge and engage with
the course through the activities and assessments experienced.
Within the interrelated components of the course, learners make sense of these experiences and
construct meaning.
We understand that because our learners may come with many different language backgrounds,
they will all progress at different paces. QMSrs values differentiated and varied instruction, which
embraces multiple learning styles. All teaching integrates the skill areas of listening, speaking,
reading, writing, viewing and presenting.
We consider all QMSrs instructors to be language instructors and the language development of our
learners to be the shared responsibility of instructors and learners. QMSrs understands that the most
conducive environment to language learning is a positive and encouraging one, wherein learners
have the opportunity to engage in authentic and meaningful learning experiences.
All teaching and learning is driven by the idea that through language, we are encouraging
intercultural awareness and new perspectives, a culture of curiosity, as well as an appreciation of
the richness of our world's diversity. We strongly believe that through this exploration of language,
learners develop as deeper, independent thinkers; as responsible, more understanding individuals;
and as better communicators in our multilingual world.

All learners:
- Are taught language by all staff and have opportunities to read, write, view, investigate, inquire
and present across the course.
- Are taught literacy skills in context.
- Are encouraged to develop a wide range of strategies to comprehend, interpret, evaluate, respond
to, appreciate and construct texts.
- Are encouraged to use self-correcting strategies.
- Have access to, and are taught through, a variety of technological and information resources.
- Are encouraged to express themselves, orally and in writing, in a variety of media and situations.
- Are encouraged to share and develop their work in a social context.
- Are encouraged to see language as a tool for thinking, inquiring, reflecting and learning.
- Are encouraged to maintain and value their mother tongue and to value that of other learners.
- Receive constructive and specific feedback from instructors and peers.
- Are given time to reflect on their learning and to apply techniques appropriately.
1.2. English and Arabic as the language of instruction at QMSrs
Primarily English and secondly Arabic is the primary language of instruction at QMSrs. Admissions
requirements and assessments ensure that all learners are able to access the course details and
material in English and Arabic. Prospective learners are advised in advance of the language in which a
course will be delivered as well as the language of material, assessment(s), project(s) and exam(s). The
use of language is clearly announced prior to acceptance to attend a course. A range of procedures is
used to assess language proficiency including but not limited to verbal language tests,
observations, communication of learner with our staff.
