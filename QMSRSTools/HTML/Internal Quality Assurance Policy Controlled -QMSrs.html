Internal Quality Assurance Policy
Aims:
- To ensure that Internal Verification (IV) is valid, reliable and covers all assessors and programme
activity.
- To ensure that the IV procedure is open, fair and free from bias.
- To ensure that there is accurate and detailed recording of IV decisions.

activity.
- To ensure that the IV procedure is open, fair and free from bias.
- To ensure that there is accurate and detailed recording of IV decisions.
In order to do this, QMSrs will:
- Ensure that all centre assessment instruments are verified as fit for purpose.
- Verify an appropriately structured sample of assessor work from all programmes, to ensure centre
programmes conform to ILM standards.
- Plan an annual internal verification schedule, linked to assignment plans.
- Define, maintain, and support effective internal verification roles.
- Ensure that identified staff will maintain secure records of all internal verification activity.
- Brief and train staff of the requirements for current IV procedures.
- Promote internal verification as a developmental process between staff.
- Provide standardised IV documentation.
- Use the outcome of internal verification to enhance future assessment practice.

Additionally, post course assignment marking will be completed by an independent assessor so as
to maintain a separation and thus obviate any chance of favouritism. There will be dip sampling of
these assessments completed by the training and programme development manager on a regular
basis. The subjective assessment examination will be marked by use of a marking guide. instructors
will be regularly assessed by either the Director, Head of Operations or Training and Programme
Development Manager attending delivered courses on an occasional basis to assure quality and
compliance with the described course content. Instructors will be provided with a prescriptive
PowerPoint presentation to guide their delivery of respective courses which they will be expected
to adhere to. Although they will not be expected to 'read-off' these PowerPoint's in their entirety and
they will add their own delivery, they will be expected to cover the learning points described for
each course and the 'assessors' will be recording these points together with comments as to style and
ease of comprehension to arrive at an overall delivery assessment. For consistency these instructor
assessments will be recorded on pro-forma sheets and retained by the organisation for future
reference.
