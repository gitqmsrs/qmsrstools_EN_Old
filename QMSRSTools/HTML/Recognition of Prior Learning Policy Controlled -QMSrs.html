Recognition of Prior Learning Policy
1. Policy Statement(s)
1) QMSrs will recognise and give credit for prior learning wherever such learning is relevant to a
QMSrs accreditation and its course and standards, irrespective of where or how the learning has
taken place.
2) This policy covers recognition of prior learning that cannot be wholly attributed to formal
courses of study successfully completed outside of the programme.
2. Explanation
The purpose of this policy is to ensure that learners are offered an opportunity to have relevant
previous learning recognised. QMSrs has a special focus on adult learners, many of whom will
already be in the workforce, and who seek to change or enhance their careers. Such learners can
often benefit by the process of assessment of prior learning (APL).
APL is a process through which individuals seek academic credit for relevant 'skills and knowledge'
they have already acquired. Relevant learning can arise from life experience, formal or informal
work experience, and/or formal and informal education or training which may or may not have
been assessed. It is important to appreciate that APL is a process to assess the learning which
results from that experience; it is not the recognition of experience alone.
This policy applies to any qualification that has a graduate profile that is cle ar, comprehensive and
usable for the purpose of APL and any course that forms part of a QMSrs programme where the
course structure and learning outcomes permit. Each programme's regulations will record the
availability of APL in the programme.

4 . Procedure
4.1. Implementation
Program committees will be responsible for implementing this policy in a way that
honours the QMSrs values:
1) integrity, honesty and accountability
2) creativity, innovation and courage
3) relevance and responsiveness
4) care, tolerance and respect
5) fairness and justice.
When applying these values to APL, particular attention is expected to be given to the
following:
4.1.1 Qualified Staff Advisors
The programme committee must ensure that staff providing advice ('APL advisors') to applicants are
appropriately qualified.
4.1.2 Cost for APL
A fee may be charged for managing the application process, as well as the assessment process. In
no case will the total fees charged to applicants exceed those for enrolment in the course of study

4.1.4 Applicant Information
APL applicants must be provided with all relevant information to assist them with the procedural

4.1.5 Assessment
Programme committees must develop, document and communicate processes for the approval of
APL, including methods of assessment to be used.
The programme committee must be satisfied that the asse ssment method(s) selected will result in
fair, valid and reliable assessment.
Separation of the roles of advice and assessment is important. For this reason the APL advisor will
not assess the work.
4.1.6 Awarding of Credits
Where full course recognition is given the applicant is granted APL credit and awarded a 'CR'
graded pass. The award of a CR grade means that 'the course has been passed through assessment
of prior learning (APL) or through the cross credit of a formal course(s) of study successfully
completed outside of the programme'.
4.1.7 Restrictions on APL Credit
APL Credit will not be given for:
1) Any qualification or courses or requirements that are deemed not eligible in the Programme
Regulations.
2) A research project, dissertation or thesis in a postgraduate programme.

4.1.9. APL and QMSrs Staff
QMSrs staff members may apply for assessment of prior learning, under the same conditions as any
other applicant and with regard to the regulations of the programme. When a QMSrs staff member
requests APL for a course(s) within a QMSrs programme in which s/he teaches, a certain proportion
of that staff member's evidence for APL must be moderated by an independent external person
before the grade is released to the applicant. The Programme Committee will receive a copy of the
external moderators report, along with the grade recommendation of the APL Assessor.
