2 Business Model
2.1 QMS Function Structure
The QMS comprises a series of inter-related documents.
At Level 1, the Quality / Operations Manual defines in practical terms how the
Quality Policy will be implemented, monitored and measured to ensure continuous
improvement in the pursuit of Service Excellence. Note: the Quality Policy may
become part of the Quality Manual.
Level 2 Business Processes contains the Business Process model which depicts the
business functions that work together to deliver products and services. Each
business area depicted has a lower level diagram that further defines the operation
and shows which Work Instruction(s) need to be carried out to complete the
operation.
At Level 3, Work Instructions are supplied to ensure that the processes are operated
in a consistent, efficient and auditable manner. This is the detail that shows how the
processes are initiated, the information input to the process, how that information is
reviewed, transformed and updated to produce the desired product or service
(deliverable). They also state what templates are required to be completed (contains
links to the templates for quick access).
Level 4 Supporting Documentation holds the results of reviews and improvement
programmes that underpin activities carried out on the QMS. It also has the
documentation that supports the use of allowable deviations from the issued QMS
processes, and Quality Records.

3.1.1 Project Summary and Objective
As the QMS implementation takes hold, the administrative and implementation
effectiveness shall be realised. This will gradually reduce the maintenance cost of
each regional QMS.
Note: local variations may be required due to legal or cultural (e.g. language,
currency) reasons or due to client requirements, client interfaces,
roles/responsibilities versus global processes, systems and tools used – but will still
reside on the QMS.
3.1.2 Business Objectives
 To have a QMS that delivers consistency and efficiency of products and services
to the clients
 To enable the QMS to be recognized as containing “best practice”
 To demonstrate that the QMS is a vehicle for continuous improvement through
the Measurement, Analysis and Improvement that initiate improvement
programmes.
3.1.3 Business Benefits
 A QMS that is accessible at globally, containing “best practice”
